# Jobs to Be Done

Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) reframes product thinking around the progress a customer is trying to make in a specific circumstance. People do not buy products — they hire them to do a job. A milkshake competes with a banana and a bagel, not just other milkshakes. Understanding the job reveals the true competitive landscape and what "better" actually means.

Apply JTBD when defining a new product, repositioning an existing one, or when customer segments based on demographics are not yielding useful insights. It is especially powerful when you are trying to understand why people switch to or from your product.

**Steps to apply:**
1. Interview customers who recently made a switch — what were they struggling with?
2. Map the job: "When I [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [desired outcome]."
3. Identify the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of the job.
4. Evaluate your product against the full job, not just feature comparisons with competitors.

**Common mistakes:** Defining jobs too narrowly (feature requests) or too broadly (be happy). Ignoring the emotional and social dimensions. Confusing the job with the solution — the job exists independent of any product.
