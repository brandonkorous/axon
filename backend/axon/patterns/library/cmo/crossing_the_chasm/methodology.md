# Crossing the Chasm

Crossing the Chasm describes the dangerous gap between early adopters (who buy vision) and the early majority (who buy proven solutions). Many products gain traction with enthusiasts but stall because pragmatists need references, whole product solutions, and low risk. The chasm is where most startups die.

Apply this pattern when early traction is slowing, when sales cycles are lengthening, or when planning the transition from product-led growth to a broader market push. It is critical during the transition from a few hundred to a few thousand customers.

**Steps to apply:**
1. Identify a single beachhead segment in the early majority — a specific niche with a compelling reason to buy.
2. Deliver the "whole product" for that niche — not just the core technology, but everything needed for a complete solution.
3. Dominate that niche to create word-of-mouth references pragmatists trust.
4. Use that beachhead to expand into adjacent segments.

**Common mistakes:** Targeting the entire mainstream market at once instead of a tight niche. Relying on the same messaging that worked for early adopters. Underinvesting in support, documentation, and integrations that pragmatists require.
