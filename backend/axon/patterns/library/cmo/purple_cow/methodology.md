# Purple Cow

The Purple Cow principle states that in a world of infinite choices and limited attention, the only viable marketing strategy is to build something worth talking about. A purple cow in a field of brown cows gets noticed. Average products with big ad budgets lose to remarkable products that spread through word of mouth. The product IS the marketing.

Apply this when planning a new product, feature, or campaign and the current direction feels safe and incremental. It is a litmus test: would someone who encounters this feel compelled to tell a friend?

**Steps to apply:**
1. Ask: "What is remarkable about this?" If the answer is nothing, go back to the drawing board.
2. Identify the "sneezers" — the early adopters in your niche who spread ideas.
3. Design the product or experience to give sneezers something worth talking about.
4. Accept that remarkable means some people will not like it. That is the cost of standing out.

**Common mistakes:** Confusing remarkable with weird or gimmicky. Playing it safe because remarkable feels risky. Trying to be remarkable to everyone instead of a specific audience. Adding remarkability as a marketing layer instead of building it into the product.
