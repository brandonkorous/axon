# Positioning

Positioning is not about what you do to the product but what you do to the mind of the prospect. In an overcommunicated world, people simplify by assigning each brand a single concept. Volvo owns "safety." FedEx owns "overnight." The goal is to claim and defend a specific word or idea in your category so that when the need arises, your brand surfaces first.

Apply this when launching a new product, entering a competitive market, or when your messaging is not landing. Positioning should be settled before any campaign, website copy, or pitch deck is created.

**Steps to apply:**
1. Define your category — what frame of reference does the customer use?
2. Identify what positions competitors already own in that category.
3. Find an open, meaningful position you can credibly claim.
4. Distill it to a single idea or word. Build all messaging outward from that anchor.
5. Be consistent — positioning is built through repetition over time, not a single campaign.

**Common mistakes:** Trying to own too many ideas at once. Picking a position that is already occupied by a stronger competitor. Choosing a position that is clever but not meaningful to the buyer.
