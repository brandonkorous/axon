# Temporal Interrogation

This pattern examines your product through three distinct time horizons of user engagement. Hour 1 is first contact — can someone understand the value and get a win immediately? Hour 6 represents early habit formation — has the user integrated your product into a workflow? Hour 100 is deep adoption — does the product become indispensable, and does it reward expertise?

Apply this when prioritizing features, diagnosing drop-off in your funnel, or debating whether to optimize onboarding versus power-user capabilities. Most products fail at one specific temporal stage, and knowing which one focuses your investment.

**Steps:** (1) Walk through your product as a brand-new user and note every friction point in the first hour. (2) Map the journey of a user who has used it a few times — what keeps them coming back? (3) Study your power users at Hour 100 — what do they wish existed? (4) Identify which stage has the worst drop-off and fix that first.

The common mistake is over-investing in Hour 1 polish while neglecting Hour 100 depth, or vice versa. Another error is assuming all three stages need simultaneous improvement — pick the bottleneck.
