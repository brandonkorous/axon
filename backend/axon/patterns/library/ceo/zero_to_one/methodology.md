# Zero to One

Zero to one means creating something that did not exist before — a new category, a new capability, a monopoly built on a secret insight. One to N means copying or incrementally improving what already exists. Thiel argues that true value creation comes from zero-to-one moves, and that competition is often a sign you are playing a one-to-N game.

Apply this pattern when evaluating new product ideas, assessing competitive positioning, or deciding whether to build versus copy. Ask: "Are we creating a new category or fighting for share in an existing one?"

**Steps:** (1) State what you are building. (2) Ask: "Does this exist in any form today?" (3) If yes, ask: "What is our secret — what do we know or believe that others do not?" (4) If you cannot articulate a clear secret, you are likely in a one-to-N space. (5) Either find the genuine zero-to-one angle or accept the one-to-N dynamics and compete on execution.

The mistake is labeling incremental work as zero-to-one to feel innovative. Another trap is pursuing novelty for its own sake — a zero-to-one idea still needs a real market.
