# Distribution Awareness

Most technical founders over-index on product quality and under-index on distribution. The best product does not win — the best-distributed product wins. Distribution means the specific, repeatable mechanism by which your product reaches users: virality, sales, SEO, partnerships, open-source community, or embedded integrations.

Apply this pattern before writing a single line of code on a new initiative. If you cannot describe how the product reaches users in one sentence, stop building and figure that out first. Also apply it when growth stalls despite a product users love.

**Steps:** (1) Name your primary distribution channel explicitly. (2) Ask: "Is this channel scalable, or does it require linear effort per customer?" (3) Check whether your product has a built-in distribution mechanic — does using it naturally expose it to new users? (4) If not, design one or accept that you need a sales/marketing engine. (5) Measure distribution efficiency as rigorously as you measure product quality.

The common mistake is assuming "build it and they will come." Another trap is spreading across many channels at once instead of dominating one before expanding.
