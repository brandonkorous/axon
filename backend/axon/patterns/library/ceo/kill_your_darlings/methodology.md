# Kill Your Darlings

Originally writing advice — cut the sentences you love most because they are probably self-indulgent — this pattern applies directly to product and business decisions. The features, projects, or strategies you are most emotionally attached to are the hardest to evaluate objectively. Sunk cost and identity attachment make leaders defend things long past their usefulness.

Apply this pattern during roadmap reviews, resource allocation, and any time you catch yourself saying "but we already invested so much in this." It is especially important when a product or feature is consuming resources disproportionate to its impact.

**Steps:** (1) List your current initiatives. (2) For each, ask: "If we were starting fresh today with no history, would we start this?" (3) If the answer is no, ask: "What would it cost to stop?" (4) Compare that exit cost to the ongoing cost of continuing. (5) Make the cut and reallocate resources to what matters most now.

The mistake is treating this as a license to abandon everything — consistency matters. The real skill is distinguishing between a darling that deserves killing and a long-term bet that needs patience.
