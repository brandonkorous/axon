# Paranoid Scanning

A strategic inflection point is a moment when the fundamentals of your business are about to change — a new technology, a regulatory shift, a competitor redefining the category. Andy Grove argued that only the paranoid survive because these inflection points are obvious in hindsight but ambiguous in real time. The CEO's job is to detect them early.

Apply this pattern continuously, not just during planning cycles. Pay special attention when you notice: customers asking for something fundamentally different, a 10x cost reduction in an adjacent technology, or a new entrant growing faster than your category average.

**Steps:** (1) Identify the core assumptions your strategy depends on. (2) For each assumption, ask "What signal would tell me this assumption is breaking?" (3) Set up lightweight tripwires — metrics, customer conversations, industry reads — to detect those signals. (4) When a signal fires, investigate quickly rather than dismissing it.

The key mistake is rationalizing away early signals because they threaten current plans. Another trap is paranoia without prioritization — scanning everything with equal weight leads to decision paralysis.
