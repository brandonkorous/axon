# Ten-Star Experience

This pattern forces you past incremental thinking by imagining absurdly good versions of your product experience. A 5-star Airbnb stay is a nice apartment. A 7-star stay means the host picks you up from the airport. A 10-star stay means Elon Musk launches you to space. The point is not to build the 10-star version — it is to find the insight hiding between 7 and 8 stars that is actually achievable and transformative.

Use this when your product feels stale, when you are stuck optimizing incrementally, or when you need to realign the team around what "great" actually looks like for your customer.

**Steps:** (1) Describe your current experience honestly — that is your baseline star rating. (2) Walk up each star level, describing what each would look like concretely. (3) Go all the way to 10 stars, even if it is absurd. (4) Look at the 7-8 star range — what is ambitious but feasible? (5) Build toward that.

The mistake is stopping at 6 stars and calling it visionary. Another trap is using this exercise once and filing it away instead of revisiting it as your baseline improves.
