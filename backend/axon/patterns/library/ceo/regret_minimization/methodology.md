# Regret Minimization Framework

This framework cuts through short-term noise by forcing a long time horizon. You imagine yourself at 80 years old looking back on your life and ask: "Will I regret not doing this?" It is designed for big, ambiguous, life-or-company-defining decisions where spreadsheets and pros/cons lists fall short.

Use regret minimization for high-stakes, high-uncertainty decisions — launching a company, pivoting a product, walking away from a lucrative but misaligned opportunity. It works best when fear of failure is the primary blocker.

**Steps:** (1) Clearly state the decision and the two paths. (2) Project forward to age 80. (3) Ask: "Will I regret not trying this, even if it fails?" (4) Ask the inverse: "Will I regret doing this if it costs me X?" (5) The path with less projected regret wins.

The main mistake is using this for every decision — it is meant for rare, pivotal moments, not daily operations. Another error is confusing excitement with regret avoidance; wanting something is not the same as regretting its absence.
