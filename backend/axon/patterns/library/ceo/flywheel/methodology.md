# Flywheel

A flywheel is a self-reinforcing loop where the output of one stage becomes the input to the next, creating compounding momentum over time. Amazon's classic flywheel: lower prices attract more customers, more customers attract more sellers, more sellers increase selection, greater selection justifies lower prices. Each push makes the next push easier.

Use this pattern when designing growth strategy, evaluating where to invest resources, or diagnosing why growth has stalled. If your activities do not feed back into each other, you are pushing a boulder, not spinning a flywheel.

**Steps:** (1) Map your core activities and their outputs. (2) Identify which outputs become inputs for other activities. (3) Draw the loop — if it does not close, you do not have a flywheel yet. (4) Find the weakest link and invest disproportionately there. (5) Resist the urge to add unrelated initiatives that break the loop.

The common mistake is drawing a flywheel that looks good on a whiteboard but has a broken link in practice. Another error is impatience — flywheels take sustained effort before momentum becomes visible.
