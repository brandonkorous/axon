# Boring Technology

Every company gets roughly three innovation tokens — novel technologies you can adopt before the accumulated operational risk becomes unmanageable. Boring technology is technology that is well-understood, widely deployed, and has known failure modes. It may not be exciting, but its failure patterns are documented and its talent pool is deep.

Apply this pattern when evaluating any new technology choice — a new database, a new language, a new framework. Before adopting it, ask: "Is this where we want to spend an innovation token?" If the answer is no, choose the boring option. Reserve your tokens for the places where novel technology gives you a genuine competitive advantage.

**Steps:** (1) List every non-standard technology in your stack. (2) Count your innovation tokens — are you already at three? (3) For the proposed new technology, identify what boring alternative exists. (4) Justify the token spend in terms of concrete business value, not developer excitement. (5) If you cannot justify it, choose boring.

The most common mistake is confusing developer enthusiasm with business value. The second mistake is underestimating operational cost — every novel technology requires its own runbooks, monitoring, hiring pipeline, and on-call expertise.
