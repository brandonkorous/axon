# Glue Work

Glue work is the invisible labor that holds teams together — onboarding new members, writing documentation, coordinating across teams, improving processes, noticing gaps and filling them. It is essential for a team to function but is often unrecognized in performance reviews because it does not show up as shipped features or closed tickets.

Apply this pattern when evaluating team health and individual contributions. If one person is doing most of the glue work, they are likely under-recognized and at risk of burnout or career stagnation. As a CTO, you need to ensure glue work is distributed, valued, and visible.

**Steps:** (1) Identify who on each team is doing glue work — onboarding, documentation, cross-team coordination, process improvement. (2) Make that work visible in planning and reviews. (3) Distribute it across the team so no single person carries the burden. (4) Ensure glue work counts toward performance and promotion. (5) If someone is doing too much glue work at the expense of technical growth, help them rebalance.

The most common mistake is letting glue work be entirely volunteer-driven, which means it falls disproportionately on people who are conscientious — often women and underrepresented minorities — without career credit.
