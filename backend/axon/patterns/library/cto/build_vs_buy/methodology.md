# Build vs Buy

The build vs buy decision is really about total cost of ownership over the lifetime of the solution, not just the upfront cost. Building gives you control and fit but costs ongoing maintenance, hiring, and opportunity cost. Buying gives you speed and proven reliability but costs vendor lock-in, customization friction, and recurring fees.

Apply this pattern whenever the team proposes building something that a third-party product already solves, or whenever a vendor solution is not quite meeting your needs. The key question is: "Is this a differentiator for our business?" If it is, build. If it is commodity infrastructure, buy.

**Steps:** (1) Define the capability you need precisely. (2) Estimate the full build cost — initial development, maintenance, on-call, documentation, hiring. (3) Estimate the full buy cost — license fees, integration effort, customization limits, switching cost. (4) Ask whether this capability is a core differentiator or commodity. (5) Build differentiators, buy commodities. (6) Re-evaluate annually as the business changes.

The most common mistake is underestimating maintenance cost when building. The second mistake is underestimating integration and customization cost when buying. Both feel cheap at first and expensive at year two.
