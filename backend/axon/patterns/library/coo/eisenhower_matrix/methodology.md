# Eisenhower Matrix

The Eisenhower Matrix divides all tasks into four quadrants based on two axes: urgency and importance. Urgent tasks demand immediate attention; important tasks contribute to long-term goals. The insight is that most people spend their time on urgent-but-unimportant work while neglecting important-but-not-urgent work, which is where strategic leverage lives.

Use this pattern when you or your team feel overwhelmed, when everything seems like a priority, or during weekly planning to ensure the important work does not get crowded out by the loud work.

**The four quadrants:**
1. **Urgent + Important** — Do it now (crises, hard deadlines).
2. **Not Urgent + Important** — Schedule it (strategy, relationships, prevention).
3. **Urgent + Not Important** — Delegate it (interruptions, most emails, some meetings).
4. **Not Urgent + Not Important** — Eliminate it (busywork, time sinks).

**Steps:** List all tasks, classify each into a quadrant, then act accordingly. The goal is to spend most time in quadrant 2.

**Common mistakes:** Treating everything as urgent. Failing to delegate quadrant 3 work. Never auditing what falls into quadrant 4 and cutting it.
