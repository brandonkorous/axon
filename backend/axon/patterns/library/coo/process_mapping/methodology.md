# Process Mapping

Process mapping is the practice of drawing out every step, handoff, decision point, and delay in a workflow before trying to improve it. You cannot optimize what you cannot see. Most process problems live in the handoffs between steps, not within any single step, and a visual map makes these gaps obvious.

Apply this pattern when onboarding to a new operation, when a process has grown organically and nobody can explain it end-to-end, or before any significant process change. It is a prerequisite to effective optimization.

**Steps to apply:**
1. Define the start and end points of the process.
2. Walk through the process with the people who actually do the work.
3. Document each step, decision, handoff, wait time, and rework loop.
4. Mark where time, quality, or information is lost.
5. Redesign by eliminating unnecessary steps, parallelizing where possible, and clarifying handoffs.

**Common mistakes:** Mapping the idealized process instead of the actual one. Skipping the people who do the work and mapping from memory or documentation alone. Jumping to solutions before the map is complete.
