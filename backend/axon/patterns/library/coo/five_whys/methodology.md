# Five Whys

The Five Whys is a root cause analysis technique developed within the Toyota Production System. When a problem occurs, you ask "why did this happen?" and then ask "why?" again of each answer, typically five times. Each layer peels back a symptom to reveal a deeper cause until you reach something structural and fixable.

Apply this whenever a problem recurs, an incident happens, or a team keeps applying band-aid fixes. It is most effective for operational and process failures where the cause chain is linear rather than multi-causal.

**Steps to apply:**
1. State the problem clearly and specifically.
2. Ask "Why did this happen?" and write down the answer.
3. Take that answer and ask "Why?" again.
4. Repeat until you reach a root cause you can act on (often 5 iterations, sometimes fewer).
5. Implement a countermeasure at the root cause level, not at the symptom level.

**Common mistakes:** Stopping too early at a comfortable answer. Branching into blame ("because Bob messed up") instead of process ("because there was no checklist"). Accepting vague answers instead of demanding specific, verifiable causes at each level.
