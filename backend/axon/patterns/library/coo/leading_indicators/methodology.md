# Leading Indicators

Leading indicators are metrics that predict future outcomes, as opposed to lagging indicators which report what already happened. Revenue is lagging; pipeline conversations are leading. Churn rate is lagging; support ticket volume is leading. The distinction matters because you can only intervene on the future, and leading indicators give you time to act.

Apply this pattern when building dashboards, setting team goals, or diagnosing why lagging metrics are off track. Every lagging metric you care about should have at least one leading indicator paired with it.

**Steps to apply:**
1. Identify the lagging outcomes that matter (revenue, retention, delivery speed).
2. For each, ask: "What upstream activity or signal predicts this outcome?"
3. Validate the correlation — does the leading indicator actually move before the lagging one?
4. Build your operating rhythm around reviewing leading indicators weekly and lagging indicators monthly.

**Common mistakes:** Measuring only lagging indicators and reacting too late. Choosing leading indicators that are easy to measure but do not actually correlate with outcomes. Gaming the leading indicator without improving the underlying behavior.
