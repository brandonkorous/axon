# Time Horizon Design

Don Norman's three levels of emotional design map to three time horizons. The visceral level (5 seconds) is the gut reaction — does it look and feel right? The behavioral level (5 minutes) is the usability — does it work well in the user's hands? The reflective level (5 years) is the meaning — does the user feel proud to own or use it? Great products succeed at all three levels.

Apply this when evaluating a design holistically or when something "works" but does not feel right (or looks beautiful but frustrates in use). Each level requires different evaluation methods.

**Steps to apply:**
1. **Visceral (5 sec)** — Show the design to someone for 5 seconds. What is their gut reaction? Does it convey quality, trust, and clarity?
2. **Behavioral (5 min)** — Have someone complete a real task. Is it efficient, predictable, and satisfying?
3. **Reflective (5 yr)** — Ask: will users recommend this? Does it align with their identity? Will it age well?
4. Identify which level is weakest and address it without sacrificing the others.

**Common mistakes:** Optimizing only for visceral appeal (pretty but unusable). Optimizing only for behavioral efficiency (functional but soulless). Ignoring the reflective level entirely, which is what builds lasting loyalty.
