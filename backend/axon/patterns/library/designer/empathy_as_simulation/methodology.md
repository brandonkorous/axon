# Empathy as Simulation

Empathy as simulation is the practice of using your own product under the degraded, distracted, and stressful conditions your users actually face. Designers typically test on fast WiFi, large screens, with full attention. Real users are on spotty mobile connections, using one hand while holding coffee, glancing at the screen while their boss walks by. Simulating these conditions reveals problems that pristine testing environments hide.

Apply this before any major release, during usability reviews, or whenever the team is confident the design "works" but has only tested it in ideal conditions.

**Steps to apply:**
1. **Bad signal** — Throttle your connection to 3G. Does the interface remain usable? Do loading states communicate progress?
2. **One hand** — Use the product with only your non-dominant thumb on a phone. Can you reach critical actions?
3. **Boss watching** — Use the product in a context where you have 5 seconds of fragmented attention. Is the key information immediately visible?
4. **Hostile data** — Test with very long names, empty fields, special characters, and extreme quantities.

**Common mistakes:** Only testing on the best hardware and fastest connections. Assuming users give the product their full attention. Skipping simulation because "we already did user testing" in a controlled lab environment.
