# Storyboard Journey

The Storyboard Journey method, inspired by Joe Gebbia's study of Disney's Snow White production process, treats the user experience as a narrative sequence. Like a film storyboard, you sketch each moment the user encounters — before, during, and after using the product. This reveals emotional peaks and valleys, transition gaps, and moments that matter far more than any wireframe can.

Apply this at the start of a project to build shared understanding, when redesigning an end-to-end flow, or when the team is too focused on individual screens and losing sight of the whole journey.

**Steps to apply:**
1. Define the starting scene (what triggers the user to begin) and the ending scene (what success looks like).
2. Sketch each key moment in between as a simple frame — include what the user sees, does, and feels.
3. Pin the storyboard on a wall and identify emotional highs, lows, and flat spots.
4. Focus design effort on amplifying the highs and eliminating or shortening the lows.

**Common mistakes:** Making the storyboard too detailed and treating it as a spec. Skipping the emotional dimension and only mapping actions. Ignoring the moments before and after the product interaction, which heavily shape perception.
